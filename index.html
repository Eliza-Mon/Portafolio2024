About me

I am a student of industrial electronics engineering, currently specializing in automotive electronics. I have a passion for programming, creating electronic projects of all kinds, and, most importantly, learning from others. Additionally, I enjoy sharing my knowledge with others.

More About Me

I cover diverse topics, including proficiency in Microsoft Office, graphing tools, Proteus, Arduino, Multisim, Solidworks, and AutoCAD. I can create and analyze various types of circuits, and I’m skilled in programming languages such as C, C++, Python, MATLAB, and Visual Basic (Excel). However, what’s most important is that I continue to acquire new abilities.

Currently, I’m interested in the Tecnolochicas project because I’m passionate about encouraging more women to explore engineering. I believe we have a lot to contribute to society as future engineers.

My experience as an electronics engineering student has been positive, even though the majority of students are male. I’m proud to say that 50% of my instructors are women, and I’ve learned a great deal from them. Both male and female professors are actively working to bridge the gender gap.
